CyberPatiot

National Cybersecurity Competition

Summary

CyberPatriot is a national youth cyber defense competition established by the Air Force Association (AFA) to inspire students towards careers in cybersecurity or other science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) disciplines. The competition primarily targets middle and high school students, offering them the opportunity to delve into the world of cybersecurity through hands-on challenges. Teams of up to six students work together to secure virtual operating systems, solve digital forensics problems, and fend off simulated cyberattacks in a controlled environment. Through multiple rounds of competition, participants enhance their skills in network security, system hardening, and incident response while competing for national recognition and scholarship opportunities.

During the competition, teams are provided with virtual images of operating systems containing vulnerabilities, which they must identify and remediate within a limited timeframe. They also face additional tasks such as configuring secure network services and responding to unexpected threats. CyberPatriot emphasizes teamwork, critical thinking, and technical proficiency as participants strive to secure their systems against a variety of cyber threats. With a blend of education, competition, and mentorship, CyberPatriot aims to develop the next generation of cybersecurity professionals equipped with the skills necessary to safeguard critical digital infrastructure.
